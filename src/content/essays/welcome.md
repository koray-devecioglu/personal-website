I wanted a site that felt like me, not a generic developer template
with a dark gradient and twelve social badges.

![Playful illustration of a notebook and terminal representing building the site in public.](/images/series/building-site-welcome.svg)

So I rebuilt it from scratch.

Not because the old one was broken. It loaded. It had pages. It had
links. Technically: website achieved.

But it did not _sound_ like me.

## What I wanted

- A writing-first site where posts are the center, not side content.
- A CV that is always current, with no PDF archaeology.
- A design that can hold both engineering and non-engineering topics
  without feeling awkward.
- A stack simple enough that future-me can maintain it without drama.

## What I did not want

- A CMS login dance.
- Cookies and consent banners for basic analytics.
- A JavaScript-heavy front page for mostly static text.
- "We'll fix it later" quality decisions.

## Ground rules

I kept a short list while building:

1. **Readable beats clever.**
2. **Fast by default.**
3. **Accessible or it does not ship.**
4. **One source of truth for content.**
5. **If it feels fake, rewrite it.**

Those rules killed a lot of "cool ideas." That was good.

## What this series covers

This short series is the behind-the-scenes story:

- how the visual system landed,
- what changed during launch week,
- and what I would do differently if I started again tomorrow.

It is intentionally short, practical, and lightly self-roasting.

Welcome aboard.
